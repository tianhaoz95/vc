📋 U.S. Crypto Tax Rules Applied

Taxable Events

Selling crypto for USD, swapping one crypto for another, and spending crypto on goods/services (e.g. card top-up) are all taxable disposals.

Non-Taxable Events

Buying crypto with fiat, transferring between your own wallets, and depositing/withdrawing fiat are not taxable events.

Short-term vs Long-term

Assets held ≤ 365 days → short-term (ordinary income rates). Assets held > 365 days → long-term (0%, 15%, or 20% depending on income).

Crypto Income

Staking rewards, referral bonuses, interest, and cashback are ordinary income at fair-market value on the date received (IRS Notice 2014-21).

Cost Basis

Your cost basis is what you paid (in USD) to acquire the crypto, including fees. Income receipts use fair-market value as their basis.

References

IRS Notice 2014-21 · Rev. Rul. 2019-24 · Rev. Proc. 2024-28 · Form 8949 · Schedule D · Schedule 1

⚖️ Cost-Basis Calculation Method

The IRS allows several methods for identifying which lots are sold. Choose the one that best suits your tax strategy. FIFO is the IRS default; LIFO and HIFO require specific identification.

📄 Crypto.com CSV Format

Export from the Crypto.com App: Accounts → History → Export icon (top right). The app generates a CSV with the following columns:

Timestamp (UTC), Transaction Description, Currency, Amount, To Currency, To Amount, Native Currency, Native Amount, Native Amount (in USD), Transaction Kind

Supported transaction kinds: crypto_purchase, crypto_exchange, crypto_earn_interest_paid, referral_gift, card_cashback_reverted, and more.
